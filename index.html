Welcome to the Dog House


This dog house is a little different on the inside, no funky smells*, no stale water bowl, no muddy paw prints just fresh beer poring from 4 taps and a fermenter sitting along side a kettle and mash tun.


At the Dog & Bone we have enough capacity to brew 38L / 10 Gal / 67 Pints at a time and keg storage for 114L / 25 Gal / 200 Pints. You can be sure to find at least 4 different kegs on tap with another two usually sitting in the wings and something bubbling away in the fermenter. Doors are open to friends, family and free-loaders alike.


*we can't gaurantee the smells, some of these hop varities stink.

Pouring Now

- PAW - 4.5% - Pale Ale -

Our go to Pale Ale, refreshing and easy drinking like a British Pale but with enough hops to confuse it with a West Coaster. A crowd pleaser just like the trick.

- Mango Sour - 4.0% - Gose -

Clean, Sharp and Fruity. A refreshing kettle soured Gose to sip in the sun, guess how many mangos went into it?.

- Sun Dog Millionare - 5.2% - Mexican Lager -

Kick back in the sun with this cripsy brew. We've added hops that make you wonder where the lime is hidden. None here, honest, just Motueka all the way from New Zealand.

- Ol' Red - 5.2% - Red IPA -

Biscuits, not that kind, but you'll know what we mean. A Red IPA with a bread like charater promoting the malt profile. Hops to bitter but no show stealers here.

Our Story

From humble begginings to big dog beers. A dynamic duo of two best friends, Rob and Gimli, set out to create a brew space of their own and in late 2019 the Dog & Bone Beer Co was born.


Chief of Biscuit Operations, G is in charge of flavour profiles, mouth feel, and quality control. With years of tasting experience behind him testing some of the finest treats on offer, you can rest asure your treats are in safe paws. Heck this guy is even the logo, that's how much we trust him.


Rob does all the hard work, from this website to drinking the produce, you know quality control and all that. I've always enjoyed cask beer and as the beer scene has grown so did my love for kegged beers. I have a particualr thing for a hoppy, fruity IPA's, a sweet spot for a sour and a devotion for the dark stuff. I wanted to learn new skills and home brewing seemed to be the perfect platform to bring them all together, building a brand, coding and of course brewing beer. So follow me and G on our journey, who nows where we will end up, the only gaurantee is there will be beer.
